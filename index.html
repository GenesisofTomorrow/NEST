About the Cluster

A unified science infrastructure for India's Northeast

The North-Eastern Science & Technology (NEST) Cluster is a Government of India initiative established under the Ministry of Science & Technology, in alignment with the National Science and Technology Cluster Policy of 2022. NEST constitutes the most comprehensive and systematically designed science governance architecture ever deployed in Northeast India.

NEST operates through a federated model: a central Secretariat in Guwahati, Assam, anchors a network of state-level cluster nodes in each of the eight northeastern states. Each node is constituted by the state government, a designated academic anchor institution, and representation from industry and civil society. This structure ensures that national scientific priorities are translated into regionally relevant programmes, and that local knowledge and needs are surfaced upward into national policy.

The Cluster draws its mandate from three strategic imperatives: first, to correct decades of underinvestment in scientific infrastructure across the region; second, to harness the Northeast's unique ecological, cultural and geographical endowments as assets for scientific inquiry; and third, to generate inclusive prosperity by connecting science and technology directly to the livelihoods of rural and tribal communities.

Governance Structure

NEST is governed by a multi-tier architecture: a National Governing Board chaired by the Secretary, DST; a Scientific Advisory Council comprising eminent researchers and technologists; eight State Cluster Management Committees; and an independent Academic Review Board. Day-to-day operations are managed by an Executive Director with a professional secretariat. All grant disbursements are subject to quarterly utilisation certification and annual independent audit.

01
Flagship — Research Funding

Research Grant Programme

The NEST Research Grant Programme is the Cluster's flagship funding mechanism for fundamental and applied research. Grants are awarded on a competitive basis across four priority domains: biotechnology and life sciences; clean and renewable energy; advanced materials and nanosciences; and artificial intelligence, quantum computing and data sciences. Project grants range from ₹25 lakhs to ₹3 crores over one to three years, with an expedited category for seed grants below ₹10 lakhs.

Grant Range ₹10L – ₹3Cr per project
Eligibility NEST-affiliated institutions; individual researchers with institutional sponsorship
Cycle Annual call; rolling seed grant window

02
Innovation — Entrepreneurship

Startup Incubation Hub

The NEST Startup Incubation Hub is a structured, 20-week cohort-based programme for technology-led startups with founders from Northeast India. The programme provides co-working infrastructure, mentorship from domain experts and industry leaders, seed grants of up to ₹50 lakhs, connections to angel investors and venture funds, and facilitated pilot partnerships with state governments and public enterprises. The Hub operates from the Guwahati node with satellite operations in Imphal and Shillong.

Seed Grant Up to ₹50L per startup
Duration 20-week intensive cohort
Cohort Size 15–20 startups per cohort

03
Inclusion — Grassroots

Rural Science Outreach

The Rural Science Outreach Programme is NEST's commitment to ensuring that scientific literacy and access to technology are not privileges of the urban elite. The programme deploys mobile science laboratories — fully equipped vehicles staffed by trained science communicators — to rural blocks, including remote tribal areas. Content is developed in 14 regional languages including Assamese, Bodo, Meitei, Mizo, Khasi, Garo, Nagamese and Nepali. Digital content is designed for low-bandwidth and offline access.

Coverage 180+ villages; 2,400+ rural touchpoints
Languages 14 regional languages
Liaisons 60 trained community liaisons deployed
Target 500 villages by FY 2027
Learn About Outreach →
04
Capacity — Academic

Fellowship & Scholar Exchange

The NEST Fellowship Programme enables researchers and students from Northeast institutions to undertake short-term attachments (2–6 weeks) and long-term fellowships (3–12 months) at premier national laboratories, IITs, IISc and CSIR centres. Reciprocal positions are offered to bring researchers from national institutions into NEST-affiliated centres. The programme also supports joint doctoral supervision between NEST partner institutions and national universities.

Fellowship Types Short-term, long-term, doctoral co-supervision
Stipend ₹30,000–₹65,000 per month
Annual Intake 120 fellows per cycle
Partners IITs, NIT, CSIR, IISER, DRDO

05
Translation — Industry

Technology Transfer Office

The NEST Technology Transfer Office (TTO) provides end-to-end support for the commercialisation of research outputs generated within the Cluster. Services include patent landscape analysis, provisional and complete patent filing support, technology valuation, licensing agreement drafting, industry matchmaking, and facilitation of government procurement of indigenously developed technologies. The TTO is staffed by IP professionals and technology managers with sectoral expertise.

Services IP filing, licensing, industry matchmaking
Patents Filed 47 patents since inception
Technologies Transferred 18 commercial licensing agreements
Turnaround 6–8 weeks for initial assessment

06
Dialogue — Policy

Science Policy Dialogue

The Annual NEST Science Policy Dialogue is a structured convening of researchers, policymakers, state government representatives, industry leaders and civil society organisations. The Dialogue is designed to surface evidence-based policy recommendations, co-create science and technology priorities specific to the Northeast, and review the performance of existing programmes against stated objectives. Outputs of the Dialogue are published as NEST Policy Briefs and submitted to the Ministry of Science & Technology.

Frequency Annual flagship + quarterly consultations

Our Region

Science that reaches every valley, every village

NEST serves a region of extraordinary diversity — eight states, 200,000 sq km, over 200 distinct tribal groups, 46 million people.

Northeast India is simultaneously one of the world's most significant biodiversity hotspots, one of South Asia's most linguistically diverse regions, and one of the most geopolitically consequential border areas in the subcontinent. It is also one of the most historically underserved in terms of scientific infrastructure and investment.

NEST's architecture accounts for this dual character. The Cluster actively harnesses the Northeast's unique scientific endowments — its high-altitude ecosystems, endemic flora and fauna, traditional ecological knowledge, geothermal potential and seismological significance — as the foundation for a distinctive regional science identity, rather than simply importing frameworks developed for other Indian contexts.

Infrastructure

World-class facilities, within reach

Eight specialised research infrastructure nodes, distributed across the NEST network and accessible to all affiliated institutions.

FAC-01

Genomics & Life Sciences Laboratory

High-throughput next-generation sequencing platforms, mass spectrometry-based proteomics, single-cell analysis, biosafety level 2 and 3 containment. Hosted at the IIT Guwahati collaborative node.

Operational — Access by application
FAC-02

Clean Energy Research Testbed

Grid-scale solar simulation, pico- and micro-hydro test rigs, biomass gasification, green hydrogen generation and storage. Designed for high-altitude, high-rainfall and remote-grid conditions unique to Northeast topography.

Operational — Sikkim & Assam nodes
FAC-03

High-Performance Computing Cluster

Petaflop-class parallel computing for climate and hydrological modelling, AI and machine learning workloads, computational chemistry and drug discovery. Connected to the National Supercomputing Mission grid.

Operational — Remote access available
FAC-04

Biodiversity & Ecology Centre

Regional herbarium with 80,000+ specimens, zoological collection, molecular systematics laboratory, field stations in four biodiversity-rich zones, and an indigenous knowledge documentation archive covering traditional ecological and medicinal practices.

Operational — Arunachal field station open
FAC-05

Fabrication & Prototyping Hub

CNC machining, multi-material 3D printing, electronics design and testing, embedded systems workspace, PCB fabrication and an industrial IoT development environment. Open to NEST-affiliated startups and researchers.

Operational — Imphal & Guwahati nodes
FAC-06

Remote Sensing & GIS Station

X-band and L-band SAR data receiving and processing infrastructure, multi-spectral optical imagery analysis, terrain and land-cover mapping, disaster early warning integration, and a regional environmental monitoring platform.

Operational — DST-NRSC collaboration
FAC-07

Seismology & Earth Sciences Observatory

Broadband seismograph network across Zones IV and V, GPS-based crustal deformation monitoring, geothermal gradient measurement stations and a regional earthquake early warning pilot node — critical given the Northeast's position on one of Asia's most seismically active fault systems.

Operational — NGRI collaborative node
FAC-08

Digital Knowledge Commons

Open-access digital repository of NEST-funded research outputs, a multilingual science library with 1.4 million curated resources, offline-capable vernacular content portals for rural access, and a traditional knowledge digital library module developed in partnership with tribal knowledge holders.

Live — nestcluster.gov.in/knowledge
